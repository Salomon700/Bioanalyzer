Pairwise Sequence Alignment

Align two sequences to identify regions of similarity. This tool helps in comparing two sequences to find the best alignment.
Global alignment: This method compares two sequences by aligning the entire length of the sequences maximizing overall similarity.
This is useful when comparing sequences that are of the same length.
Local alignment: Instead of alinging the entire length, only regions of high density of matches are aligned.
This is useful for identifying short conserved regions in protein/nucleotide sequences. Applications

  • Identifying conserved regions in protein/nucleotide sequences
  • Identify homologous sequences
  • Pin point mutations in sequences
  • Identify suitable PCR primers
NCBI Search

Search the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI) databases for a wide range of biological data. This tool allows users to access various databases including nucleotide sequences, protein sequences, and more. Features:

  • Search for nucleotide sequences
  • Search for protein sequences
  • Access to genomic data
  • Retrieve scientific literature
  • Find information on genes and their functions
Applications:
  • Researching gene functions and structures
  • Identifying protein sequences and structures
  • Accessing scientific publications
  • Exploring genomic data for various organisms
  • Finding information on genetic variations
